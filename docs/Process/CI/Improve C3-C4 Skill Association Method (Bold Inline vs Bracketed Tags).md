## 1. What is the problem or opportunity?

During the refinement of CV bullets, we observed that attempting to force **bold inline integration** of skills (by rephrasing bullets to include skill-related words in bold) presents two issues:

- Limited positive impact on ATS matching, since ATS primarily rewards the presence of relevant keywords rather than formatting.
- Potential inconsistency and reduced readability for human readers when mixing bold inline skills with bracketed tags at the end of bullets.

## 2. What would the improvement look like?

Establish a clearer and more consistent rule for skill association in C3 and C4:

- **Primary method**: Use **bracketed tags at the end** of the bullet as the default and most consistent approach. When possible, use language that is close to the Job Lead’s requirement wording.
- **Secondary method**: Use **bold inline** selectively and only when it can naturally highlight an important part of the bullet that supports the required skill. The goal is to help human readers, not to force keyword inclusion.
- Avoid mixing both methods heavily within the same role or project to maintain visual and stylistic consistency.

Q1: How to apply selective bold inline now?

Given your preference, here’s how I propose we do it:

**Proposed Method (Selective & Light):**

- We will **not** try to bold the skill name itself in most cases.
- Instead, we will use **bold selectively** on the **most important part** of the bullet that best demonstrates the required skill. This helps the human reader quickly connect the evidence to the requirement.
- We will **keep the bracketed tag** at the end (using language from the Job Lead) for consistency.
- We only apply bold when it **adds clarity** for the reader — not on every bullet.

**Example:**

**Before (current):** Co-led the design and execution of a multi-year Wind Down programme by developing the Project Plan and Budget with input from 10 department heads, ensuring regulatory, operational, legal, and financial closure under tight shareholder deadlines and Supervisory Board oversight. _(Cross-Functional Leadership, Strategic Execution)_

**After (Proposed selective bold):** Co-led the design and execution of a multi-year Wind Down programme by developing the Project Plan and Budget with input from **10 department heads**, ensuring regulatory, operational, legal, and financial closure under tight shareholder deadlines and Supervisory Board oversight. _(Cross-Functional Leadership, Strategic Execution)_

This way, the bold highlights the **scale and complexity** (which supports the skill), while the bracket keeps the skill reference clear and consistent.
