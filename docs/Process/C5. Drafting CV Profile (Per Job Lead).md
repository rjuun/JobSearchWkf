## Purpose
Create a concise, high-impact **Profile** (also called Professional Summary or Executive Summary) tailored to each specific Job Lead.

The Profile should:

- Immediately signal **seniority** and leadership scope.
- Address the **core requirements** of that particular Job Description.
- Incorporate the strongest Keep evidence from C2 (and relevant newly added competences/attributes where they add value).
- Position the candidate as a strong fit while remaining truthful and professional.

Unlike a generic profile, this version is **Job Lead-specific** and should be ready to use (or lightly adapted) in the final CV for that application.

---

## A. Execution Sequence

1. Start from the **final cleaned C2 export** for the specific Job Lead.
2. Identify the **core requirements** (especially Rank 1 Core requirements) and the strongest supporting evidence.
3. Review any newly added **Competences** and **Attributes** from the enrichment process that are relevant to this Job Lead.
4. Draft the Profile following the [[C4. Drafting the CV Profile (Per Job Lead)#B. Profile Writing Principles|Profile Writing Principles]].
5. Keep the Profile concise (typically 4–7 lines / 70–110 words).
6. Produce one tailored Profile per Job Lead.
7. Store the Profile in a way that it can be easily retrieved and used when preparing the final CV for that application.

---

## B. Profile Writing Principles

When drafting the Profile for a specific Job Lead, apply the following principles:

### 1. Lead with Seniority and Scope

- Clearly communicate level of experience, leadership scope, and exposure to senior stakeholders (Management Board, Supervisory Board, CxO level).
- Examples of signals to include (when supported by evidence):
    - Years of experience in relevant domains
    - Direct or close interaction with Management Board / Supervisory Board
    - Leadership of cross-functional or multi-country initiatives
    - End-to-end ownership of complex transformation or governance programmes

### 2. Address the Core Requirements of the Specific Job Lead

- Prioritise the **Core requirements** (Rank 1) identified in C2.
- Weave in the strongest evidence without listing requirements mechanically.
- The reader should feel that the candidate’s background aligns well with the most important demands of _this specific role_.

### 3. Use Strong, Senior-Level Language

- Favour strategic and leadership-oriented language over purely operational descriptions.
- Use powerful but credible phrasing (e.g., _Led_, _Directed_, _Spearheaded_, _Drove_, _Owned_, _Partnered with_, _Advised_).

### 4. Balance Breadth and Depth

- Show both **range** (ability to operate across functions, countries, or complex environments) and **depth** (ownership, impact, and results in key areas).
- Avoid generic statements that could apply to many candidates.

### 5. Incorporate Relevant New Competences/Attributes (when appropriate)

- If a newly added competence or attribute (e.g., _Acting as Mediator under pressure_, _Driving initiatives with limited support_, _Persistence under high scrutiny_) is particularly relevant to the Job Lead, consider including a subtle reference.
- Only do this when it strengthens the narrative — do not force it.

### 6. Keep it Concise and Readable

- Target 4–7 lines.
- Use short paragraphs or a single well-structured block.
- The Profile should be easy to read quickly and should encourage the reader to continue to the experience section.

---

## C. Output Format

**Primary Output** One tailored **Profile** per Job Lead, stored in a clear and retrievable format (e.g., in the Job Lead folder or in a dedicated “Profiles” section of the working file).

**Recommended Structure** (flexible):

- Opening sentence: Seniority + core expertise area + key differentiator.
- Middle section: Highlight of strongest alignment with the Job Lead’s core requirements (drawing from C2 evidence).
- Closing: Leadership scope, stakeholder level, or personal attribute that reinforces fit (when relevant).

**Optional:** Create 1–2 slight variations of the Profile for the same Job Lead if different emphases are useful (e.g., one more governance-focused, one more transformation-focused), and clearly label them.

---

## D. Quality Rules

- The Profile must be based on **Keep evidence** from the final C2 export.
- It must feel **authentic** to the candidate’s actual experience and voice.
- Avoid over-claiming or using language that cannot be supported in the experience section.
- The Profile should work well both for **ATS** (clear keywords) and for **human readers** (engaging and credible).
- When tailoring for a specific Job Lead, resist the temptation to make the Profile too narrow — it should still reflect the candidate’s overall strength while emphasising fit for that role.

---

### Notes

- C4 is one of the highest-impact steps in the application process. A strong, well-tailored Profile can significantly increase the chances of the CV being read in detail.
- Because C4 produces **per-Job Lead** Profiles, it is more time-intensive than a single generic profile but delivers much higher relevance.
- The quality of the Profile depends heavily on the quality of the evidence selection done in C2. Weak or diluted evidence in C2 will result in a weaker Profile.
