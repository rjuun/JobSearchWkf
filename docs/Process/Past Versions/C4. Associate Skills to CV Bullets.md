## Purpose
Link the Skills identified for each Job Lead (from Step B5) to the CV bullets in a way that reinforces the connection between the candidate’s experience and the required skills.

This step aims to:

- Make it easier for **human readers** to see how specific skills are demonstrated through experience.
- Maintain strong **ATS compatibility** by keeping a dedicated Skills section at the top of the CV.
- Ensure that Skills associated with **Core and Important** requirements are visibly represented in the Professional Experience section.

---

## A. Execution Sequence

1. Retrieve the list of Skills for the Job Lead from the **“Skills”** column in the final C2 export (originally populated in B5).
2. Separate the Skills into two groups:
    - **Core + Important Skills** (must appear at least once in the bullets).
    - **Nice-to-Have Skills** (appear if they fit naturally).
3. For each CV bullet created in C3, evaluate whether any of the Job Lead’s Skills can be associated with it.
4. Apply the association rules in the following order of preference:
    - Integrate the Skill **in bold** inside the bullet when it fits naturally.
    - If natural integration is not possible, add the Skill as a **short bracketed tag** at the end of the bullet.
5. Ensure that every **Core and Important** Skill appears at least once across all bullets (either in bold or in brackets).
6. Review the full set of bullets for visual consistency and readability.
7. Produce the updated table with the associated Skills clearly visible.

---

## B. Skills Association Rules

### 1. Dedicated Skills Section (Top of CV)

- A dedicated **Skills section** will be created between the Profile and Professional Experience at step [[C6. Compile Complete CV Document]],.
- This section will contain the full list of Skills extracted/mapped for that specific Job Lead from B5.
- This section will serve as the primary source for ATS parsing and gives human readers a quick overview of relevant skills.

### 2. Association Methods (in order of preference)

**Method A – Bold Inline Integration (Preferred)**

- When a Skill fits naturally into the bullet, integrate it directly into the sentence and format it in **bold**.
- Example: _“Led **process harmonization** and standardization across six European countries, establishing a single Procurement and Outsourcing Procedure.”_

**Method B – Bracketed Tag at the End**

- Use this method only when the Skill cannot be naturally integrated into the bullet text.
- Add the Skill in **short bracketed form** at the end of the bullet.
- Example: _“Reduced month-end close from 20 to 5 days (Process Automation).”_
- Keep bracketed tags concise (ideally 1–3 words).

### 3. Coverage Rule for Core and Important Skills

- Every Skill associated with **Core and Important** requirements must appear **at least once** in the Professional Experience section.
- Prioritize **natural integration** (bold text inside the bullet).
- If natural integration is not possible, declare the Skill using a **bracketed tag** at the end of the bullet.
- Only if neither option is feasible should the Skill remain visible only in the top Skills section.

### 4. Nice-to-Have Skills

- These skills should be integrated when they fit naturally.
- They do **not** need to appear in the bullets if doing so would force unnatural phrasing.

---

## C. Output Format

**Primary Output** An updated version of the C3 table with one additional column:

**Associated Skills**

**Columns (recommended order):**

Lead · Lead:ID · Requirement Order · Rank · Requirement Group · Requirement · Requirement Description · Skills · Reference · Original Text · CV Position · CV Bullet · **Associated Skills**

**Rules:**

- The **Associated Skills** column shows how the skill is represented in that bullet (either as bold text or as a bracketed tag).
- If multiple skills are associated with one bullet, list them clearly (separated by commas or semicolons).

---

## D. Quality & Consistency Rules

- Always prioritize **natural integration** over forced placement.
- Keep bracketed tags short and consistent in style across the CV.
- Avoid overusing bracketed tags, as they can reduce readability and professionalism.
- Ensure visual and stylistic consistency (e.g., same format for all bracketed tags).
- The top Skills section and the bullets must work together — the bullets should reinforce, not contradict, the Skills section.
- After completing the associations, perform a final check to confirm that all **Core and Important** Skills appear at least once.

---

### Notes

- This step (C4) builds directly on the output of **C3 (Transform Evidence into CV Bullets)**.
- The approach deliberately balances **human readability** with **ATS compatibility** by maintaining a clean Skills section at the top while creating explicit connections in the experience section.
- The initial implementation will follow the coverage rule for Core and Important Skills. This can be reviewed and relaxed later if the use of bracketed tags becomes excessive or visually cluttered.
- Note for future improvement: The “Skills” column currently populated in B5 is an interpretation against the candidate’s pre-defined skill list rather than a direct extraction from the Job Description. This will be reviewed and potentially refined after C4 is completed.
