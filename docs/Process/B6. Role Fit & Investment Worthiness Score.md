## Purpose

Evaluate how well a Job Lead aligns with the candidate’s actual profile, experience level, and strengths.
This step replaces the previous [[B6. Run Initial ATS Matching Rating]] ATS-heavy scoring (retired version) and produces a more balanced assessment of whether the role is worth investing tailoring time in.

It uses the **Master Bullet Bank** (`tbl_Bullet_Bank` in `Profile_Reference_Workbook.xlsx`) as the primary reference and generates a structured **Role Fit Score** that considers both requirement coverage and broader strategic fit.

---

## A. Input Documents

For each Job Lead, review the following:

1. **Master Bullet Bank** — located in `Profile_Reference_Workbook.xlsx` → `tbl_Bullet_Bank` tab. This contains the candidate’s strongest 23 bullets with clear tagging.
2. **Job Requirements** table — from `[[Job Hunting Lists.xlsx]]`, filtered by the specific Job Lead.
3. **JD .md file** (optional) — only when additional context is needed to interpret a requirement.

Do **not** use a tailored CV at this stage. Scoring must be based on the Master Bullet Bank to ensure consistency and reduce tailoring bias.

---

## B. Scoring Dimensions

Each Job Lead is scored across five dimensions. These dimensions are designed to answer:

> “Is this role a good strategic fit, and is it worth investing time to tailor for?”

| Dimension                        | Weight | What it evaluates                                      | Source                          | Scoring Guidance |
|----------------------------------|--------|--------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|------------------|
| **Relevance to the Role**        | 35%    | Overall alignment with the nature, scope, and environment of the role | Master Bullet Bank + qualitative judgment | Highest weight. Consider role type, industry exposure, ways of working, and strategic scope. |
| **Seniority & Strategic Level**  | 20%    | Level of responsibility, exposure to leadership, and strategic scope | Master Bullet Bank              | Evaluate Board/CEO exposure, team leadership, cross-functional influence, and decision-making authority. |
| **Impact & Results**             | 20%    | Strength and clarity of outcomes and achievements      | Master Bullet Bank              | Favour bullets with clear, preferably quantified results. |
| **Requirement Alignment**        | 15%    | How well the profile covers the specific JD requirements | Per-Requirement Scoring (from B5 logic) | Derived from the weighted average of Core / Important / Nice-to-Have requirements. |
| **ATS Compatibility**            | 10%    | Closeness of language between JD and Master Bullet Bank | Qualitative assessment          | Assess how much rewording would be needed. Lower weight because it can often be improved during tailoring. |

**Overall Role Fit Score** = Weighted average of the five dimensions (rounded to 1 decimal place).


---

### B.1 Per-Requirement Scoring Rules

#### 1 Match Strength Scale

| Match Strength | Meaning                                                                           | Score range |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| Excellent      | Bullet Bank addresses this requirement fully and explicitly, with strong keywords | 9–10        |
| Very Strong    | Bullet Bank addresses it clearly; minor phrasing or depth gap                     | 7–8         |
| Good           | Bullet Bank covers it partially; evidence is present but not prominent            | 5–6         |
| Weak           | Bullet Bank has tangential or indirect evidence only                              | 2–4         |
| No Match       | No relevant evidence in the Bullet Bank                                           | 0–1         |

Match Strength and Score must be **consistent** — do not assign "Excellent" with a score of 6 or "Weak" with a score of 8.

#### 2 Per-Requirement Score (0–10)

For each requirement:
- Map the requirement to the strongest available evidence in the **Master Bullet Bank**.
- When relevant, also reference `tbl_Education` and `tbl_Language` from `Profile_Reference_Workbook.xlsx` (e.g. for academic or language-related requirements).
- Quote or reference the exact bullet text where possible.
- Score honestly — never inflate scores.
- Clearly note any gap in the `Initial_Missing_Weak` column.
- If no relevant evidence exists, mark it as `No Match` and state what is missing.

**ATS Keyword Check**: Verify whether the JD terminology appears in the Bullet Bank (or Education/Languages tables).
Significant mismatches should be noted, even if the underlying experience exists — note them in `Initial_Missing_Weak`.

#### 3 Final Per-Requirement Alignment Score (0–10)

Calculate the weighted average of all per-requirement scores:

| Requirement Group | Weight |
| ----------------- | ------ |
| Core              | 3      |
| Important         | 2      |
| Nice-to-Have      | 1      |

`Requirement Alignment Score = Σ(score × weight) ÷ Σ(weight)`

Round to one decimal place.

## C. Execution Sequence

For each Job Lead in the batch:

1. Read the Job Requirements and the Master Bullet Bank (`tbl_Bullet_Bank`).
2. Score each requirement individually following **B.1 Per-Requirement Scoring Rules**.
3. Assess the five scoring dimensions, giving particular attention to **Relevance to the Role**.
4. Calculate the Overall Role Fit Score.
5. Produce a qualitative summary including:
   - Key strengths of the fit
   - Main gaps or concerns
   - Clear recommendation on whether to proceed with tailoring

## E. Notes & Quality Rules

- Be honest and conservative. Do not inflate scores to justify pursuing a role.
- “Relevance to the Role” should carry the highest weight and should reflect strategic and contextual fit, not just requirement coverage.
- ATS Compatibility has the lowest weight because language gaps can often be addressed during tailoring.
- Always record which version of the Master Bullet Bank was used.
- Flag any requirement that was scored on weak or indirect evidence.
