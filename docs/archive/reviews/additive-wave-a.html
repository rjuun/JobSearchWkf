✨ The one-line

Three additive surfaces now sit next to what already ships — the interview brief beside the CV, a "This week" triage strip above the board, and the coach's designed stop card — each wired to a reaction signal. Nothing was removed or replaced. A tiny ux_events log records how you react, so the plan's "fold decisions" get made on evidence, not taste.

✅ What shipped (Wave A)

A1
Interview Brief — a panel bolts on beside the CV-ready download. It re-projects what the lead already holds (your kept evidence + the JD must-haves) into the next moment: proof to lead with, probes to expect, thin spots to bridge honestly. No new data, no LLM call, nothing invented.
A2
Session stop card — after 3 answered coach prompts a recap card renders (already built in the coach queue); Wave A adds only its reaction signal. "Keep going" leaves the old infinite behaviour one click away.
A3
"This week" strip — the two leads most worth acting on now, ranked by fit × freshness × saturation (all three already captured at B1), sit above the full board table, which is untouched. Hidden until there are ≥3 scored leads, so it only appears once it's actually narrowing.
◆
Instrumentation — one append-only ux_events table (surface · event · lead_id? · ts). This is the "see their reaction" mechanism; without it the additive strategy can conclude nothing.

📡 Reaction signals emitted

surface · eventanswers the fold question
interview_brief · open / printIs the interview moment the emotional peak (vs. the apply moment)?
this_week · pick_openDo users act on the picks, or free-roam the table?
coach_session · session_complete / keep_goingShould the "N to go" counts retire in favour of a bounded set?

Recording is best-effort — a telemetry hiccup can never surface as a user-facing error or block a flow.
