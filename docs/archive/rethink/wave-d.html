R1 · Interview Armament #2c #3c

The apply moment isn't the last moment

The A1 teaser already reframed kept evidence as interview prep, but ended with "the full interview workspace comes later." R1 is that workspace: a print-optimised night-before brief on its own route — proof points to lead with, must-haves to expect, honest bridges for the thin spots, and the "left out, on purpose" answers. Nothing is invented; it re-projects the C2 mapping the lead already holds.

  • uicomponents/roleproof/interview-brief.tsx — full brief, print + per-requirement expand
  • routeapp/roleproof/leads/[id]/brief/page.tsx — teaser CTA now links here

R2 · Excavation, surfaced #4c

Rediscovery that never elbows ahead of real work

The engine5 excavation prompts existed and were flagged on, but rendered inside the ranked queue where a low-value rediscovery prompt could still auto-advance into the stage. R2 pulls them out entirely: readQueue excludes them, and a new readExcavationInvites surfaces them as a distinct card below the queue — each naming a specific thin era. Accept loads it into the stage (scrolls into view); "not now" snoozes via the existing skip.

  • readlib/coaching-queue.ts — filter + readExcavationInvites; engine5 sourceRef now carries era/company
  • uicomponents/coach-queue.tsx — ExcavationInvites section, accept/snooze
  • seedscripts/seed.ts — one deliberately thin era so an invitation is always demonstrable
Fold question → Do invitations earn their place, or does the never-done meter alone carry rediscovery? Watch accepted / shown.

R3 · The Statement's re-entry ritual #2b #2e

Something that brings you back

The Statement page rendered the stream but nothing triggered a return. R3 adds a per-owner "last seen" marker (profiles.statement_seen_at) and a board banner that rolls up everything that accrued since — "since you were last here: 3 pieces of evidence kept, 2 CVs tailored and 1 more." Viewing the Statement or dismissing the banner stamps the marker. The email is a thin adapter over the same digest, deliberately last and off.

  • schemalib/db/schema.ts + drizzle/0016_*.sql — statement_seen_at column
  • liblib/activity.ts — digestSince, marker read/write, pure digestHeadline (tested)
  • uicomponents/statement-return-banner.tsx — board banner
  • emaillib/email/statement.ts — thin adapter behind NEXT_STATEMENT_EMAIL, no trigger yet
Fold question → Does statement-as-ritual drive real return sessions, replacing generic notifications? Watch banner_open → session attribution.

Decisions to review

Postponed judgment calls, surfaced here rather than silently baked in — per the plan's rule to pre-decide the fold and flag the rest.

deploy
Migration 0016 must run on the live DB.

npm run db:migrate adds profiles.statement_seen_at. Reads are defensive (they degrade to "no banner" if the column is missing), so the app won't crash if the migration lags — but the banner stays dark until it's applied on Supabase.

confirm
The seeded "thin era" is demo fixture data.

R2 seeds one early role (Financial Analyst · early career, no stories) so an excavation invitation is always demonstrable. It's demo-only and carries no fabricated employer or dates, but the demo tenant imports your real profile — replace it with your real thin era, or drop it, before this reads as history. Guarded + idempotent in shapeDemoTenant().

confirm
The demo backdates the last-Statement-seen marker 40 days.

So the re-entry banner shows on first demo load (all seeded activity reads as "new since"). Real users get the marker stamped the first time they open /statement — harmless, but noted.

deferred
The Statement digest email has no trigger.

Per the plan, email ships "only once the banner earns opens." The adapter and flag exist; a monthly cron (Vercel Cron → Resend) is the next step if banner_open proves the ritual works.
