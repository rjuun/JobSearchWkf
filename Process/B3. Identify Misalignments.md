## A. Values and Motives

A **Values and Motives Misalignment** occurs when signals in the JD conflict with the candidate's core values as defined in the following **Mandatory Input File**: [[Values & Motives Summary]]

Flag when the JD shows **explicit evidence** of:
**Work culture red flags:**
- Strictly on-site only (no hybrid or remote mention):
	- Culture: On-site only
- Language suggesting burnout culture ("always on," "high-pressure," "24/7 availability")
	- Culture: Burnout signals
- Explicit KPI-only or individual performance framework with no collaborative language
	- Culture: Non-cooperative

**Ethical/purpose red flags:**
- Industry or products conflicting with ecological, social or ethical values (e.g. weapons, extractive industries, exploitative consumer practices)
	- Ethics: [specific concern]
- Role is purely process/compliance execution with no strategic or meaningful scope
	- Purpose: Transactional only

**Leadership red flags:**
- Explicit top-down, non-participatory structures ("reports to and executes directives of")
	- Leadership: Top-down

**Important:** Most misalignments at this stage are tentative. The JD is a marketing document. Flag, proceed, and validate during company research or interview stages. A misalignment flag does **not** stop the process — it is a conscious awareness marker.

## B. Cities Preferences

A **City Misalignment** occurs when the required work location falls outside the candidate's accepted cities list, as defined in `tbl_Locations` from `Job Hunting Lists.xlsx`.

- **Primary cities** (Vienna, Amsterdam, Copenhagen, London, Milan) — no misalignment flag.
- **Secondary cities** (Barcelona, Madrid, Hamburg, Berlin, Stuttgart, München, Salzburg, Zurich, Stockholm, Dublin) — flag as
	- `City: [name] (Secondary — acceptable)`.
- **Last Option** (Budapest, Toronto, São Paulo) — flag as
	- `City: [name] (Last Option — proceed with caution)`. 
- **Not listed** — flag as 
	- `City: [name] (Not in scope)`.

Note: hybrid/remote roles with occasional travel to a non-primary city are not automatic misalignments — apply judgment on degree of physical presence required.

## C. Seniority Mismatch

A **Seniority Misalignment** occurs when the role's target experience level is 
materially different from the candidate's profile (15+ years, senior leadership).

**Overqualified — detection signals:**
- Explicit years of experience stated materially below 15 
  (e.g., "3–5 years", "4–7 years")
- Role descriptors signalling early/mid-career: "first leadership role", 
  "leadership development programme", "Associate" (non-senior level)
- Salary range disclosed well below senior market level
- Reporting structure: role reports to a position typically held by 
  someone at the candidate's own level
→ Flag as: `Seniority: Overqualified ([specific signal])`

**Underqualified — detection signals:**
- Role requires deep domain-specific experience the candidate 
  cannot credibly demonstrate (e.g., "10+ years in CIB operations", 
  "15+ years in manufacturing PMO")
- Mandatory industry certifications outside profile scope
→ Flag as: `Seniority: Underqualified ([specific signal])`

**Important:** A Seniority Misalignment does **not** stop the process — it is 
a conscious awareness marker. Proceed if the role is otherwise compelling, 
but validate fit explicitly during the interview stage.
If the underqualification is severe and the hiring team is likely to screen 
out the application at CV level, consider treating it as a functional Roadblock.

## D. Misalignment Flagging Procedure

**This is the operational rule that governs HOW misalignments are recorded.**

Whenever you identify a **Values and Motives**, **Seniority Mismatch**, or a **Cities Preferences**, you must:

1. **Write the Misalignment(s)** into the **“Misalignment” column** of the corresponding Job Description entry in the Format of the Job Leads SharePoint list.
2. Use a **clear, structured format**, such as:
    - City: Madrid (Secondary — acceptable)
    - Culture: On-site only
    - Ethics: Extractive industry
    - Seniority: Overqualified (role targets 4–7 years / first leadership role)

**Recording when no Misalignments are found:**

If you complete the Misalignment analysis and **no Misalignments** are identified, write **`None`** in the `Misalignments` column.

Do **not** leave the column blank in this case, as an empty field should indicate that the analysis has not yet been performed.
