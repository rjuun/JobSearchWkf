## A. Obligatory Language Requirements

A **Language Roadblock** occurs when a job description requires a level of language proficiency that I **do not currently possess**, based on the information in my `tbl_Languages` from [[Profile_Reference_Workbook.xlsx]].
### 1. German Language Requirements
Although I hold a **C1 Zertifikat**, my practical German level is **Business Conversational**. This means:
- I can operate professionally in German
- but I may struggle with **complex communication**, **nuanced leadership language**, or **high‑stakes stakeholder interactions**

Therefore:
- If a job description requires **native‑level**, **near‑native**, **excellent written German**, or **leadership‑grade German**, → **Flag this as a Language Roadblock.**

### 2. Spanish Language Requirements
Although I hold only a **B2 Certification**, my practical Spanish level is **Business-level Spanish**. For Portuguese speakers, it is quite easy to participate in Business Meetings without a problem. This means:
- I can fully operate professionally in Spanish

### 3. Other Languages
If a job description requires a language that is **not listed** in my `tbl_Languages`, → **Flag this as a Language Roadblock.**

## B. Obligatory Technical Requirements

A **Technical Roadblock** occurs when a job description requires a specific **software or platform** that is **not currently listed** in my `tbl_Skills_Master` from [[Profile_Reference_Workbook.xlsx]].

When you identify such a missing requirement:
1. Flag it explicitly as a **Technical Roadblock** and name the software/platform.
2. Compare the requirement against the following list of **Missing Technical Requirements** (skills I do _not_ possess). Any strict requirement for these must always be treated as a Roadblock.

### List of Missing Technical Requirements
1. SAP
2. Salesforce

> [!WARNING] ACCURACY IMPROVEMENT TIP
>  **1. Updating the Missing Requirements List**
>  If you detect a software/platform that is _not_ in my `tbl_Skills_Master` **and** is _not_ in the “List of Missing Technical Requirements,” and it appears frequently in job descriptions, ask me:
>  **“Would you like me to amend the List of Missing Technical Requirements?”**
> 
> **2. Updating the Skills_Master**
> If I confirm that I actually have experience with a software/platform that you flagged as a Technical Roadblock, ask me: 
> **“Should I update your** `tbl_Skills_Master` **to record this technical capability?”**

## D. Geographic Delivery Scope

A **Geographic Scope Roadblock** occurs when a role requires mandatory delivery, travel or territorial responsibility in regions outside acceptable scope — regardless of where the office is located.
Flag as:
	- Geographic: [Region] (mandatory delivery/travel scope)


## E. Industry-Specific Experience

An **Industry Roadblock** occurs when a job description mandates deep, domain-specific experience in an industry the candidate has not worked in, and where that experience is a core hard requirement — not a preference.

**Detection signals:**
- Explicit language: "several years of experience in the [X] industry", "extensive background in [sector]", "deep knowledge of [industry-specific concepts]" in the requirements section (not the preferred qualifications)
- The role's core tasks require industry knowledge that is non-transferable without direct sector experience (e.g., OEM quotation cost structures in automotive, GxP manufacturing compliance in pharmaceuticals)
- The company is a sector specialist where all roles presuppose industry fluency (e.g., automotive Tier-1 supplier, defence contractor, upstream oil & gas)

**Industries NOT in the candidate's profile (hard industry roadblocks when explicitly required):**
1. Automotive (OEM / Tier-1 supplier manufacturing)
2. Defence / Aerospace
3. Mining / Resources extraction
4. Oil & Gas (upstream / midstream)
5. Pharmaceuticals / Life Sciences (GxP manufacturing context only)


> [!NOTE] Industries within profile — not roadblocks
> The following Industries are within the candidate's profile:
> - Financial Services,
> - Banking,
> - Insurance,
> - SaaS / Tech,
> - Consulting,
> - FMCG,
> - Industrial / Manufacturing (general finance roles), and
> - Shared Services
>
> Finance or transformation roles in these sectors are **not** Industry Roadblocks even if industry experience is preferred.


> [!WARNING] **ACCURACY IMPROVEMENT TIP**
> If you identify an industry not listed above as explicitly required and the candidate has no demonstrable experience in it, ask:
> **"Should I add [industry] to the Industry Roadblock list?"**

Flag as:
`Industry: [Sector name] (mandatory sector experience)`

Examples:
- `Industry: Automotive (mandatory OEM supplier experience)`
- `Industry: Defence (active security clearance required)`

## F. Roadblock Flagging Procedure

**This is the operational rule that governs HOW Roadblocks are recorded.**

Whenever you identify a **Language**, **Technical**, **Geographical**, **Certification** or **Industry Specific** Roadblock, you must:

1. **Write the Roadblock(s)** into the **“Roadblocks” column** of the corresponding Job Description entry in the Format of the Job Leads SharePoint list.
2. Use a **clear, structured format**, such as:
    - Language: German (Leadership-level requirement)
    - Technical: SAP
    - Certification: CFA

3. If multiple Roadblocks exist, list them separated lines:
    - Technical: SAP
    - Certification: CPA, ACCA
    - Language: German (native-level required), Arabic
    - Geographic: Middle East (mandatory delivery scope)
    - Industry: Automotive (mandatory OEM supplier experience)

**Recording when no Roadblocks are found:**

If you complete the Roadblock analysis and **no Roadblocks** are identified, write **`None`** in the `Roadblocks` column.

Do **not** leave the column blank in this case, as an empty field should indicate that the analysis has not yet been performed.
