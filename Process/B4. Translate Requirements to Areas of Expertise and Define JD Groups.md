## Context and Origin

This step was designed based on an initial exercise with 110 Job Descriptions.

The 110 positions were categorised into 6 JD Groups:
1. Strategy & Corporate Development
2. Chief of Staff & Executive Office
3. Operations & Shared Services
4. Controlling / FP&A / Finance
5. Transformation & Project Management
6. Procurement / Outsourcing / ESG

From this exercise, 12 Areas of Expertise were initially identified:
A. Strategic Planning | Corporate Development
B. Corporate Governance
C. Operations | Back-Office | Shared Services
D. Controlling
E. Financial Planning and Analysis
F. Project Management
G. Process Management
H. Procurement | Outsourcing
I. ESG | Sustainability
J. Change | Transformation Management | Merger Integration
K. Reporting and Analytics (Regulatory and Performance)
L. Banking | Financial Services

An AI Agent then ran a detailed analysis of each of the 110 JD PDF files to:
1. Reassess each AoE rating
2. Comment on possible rating changes vs. the initial superficial rating
3. Provide Key Patterns & CV Tailoring Notes

From this in-depth analysis, 5 additional Areas of Expertise were
identified as significant and added to the framework:
M. Leadership & People Management
N. ERP / SAP / Digital Systems
O. German Language Proficiency
P. Consulting Pedigree / Ex-Consultant Toolkit
Q. Regulatory & Compliance Expertise

The 6 JD Groups were maintained, with the understanding that most JDs belong
to a dominant Primary Group but also exhibit characteristics of a Secondary Group.

Letter codes A–Q were added to the Areas of Expertise columns in
`Job Hunting Lists.xlsx` (row 2) to serve as a consistent shorthand reference.

## Purpose

For each Job Lead that has passed Steps B.1–B.3, translate the Job Description into the 17-Areas of Expertise framework, assign a JD Group classification, and produce structured outputs ready for entry into the `Job Leads` SharePoint list.

Step outputs feed: `JD Group Primary`, `JD Group Secondary`, 17 Area of Expertise ratings, `Key Patterns & CV Tailoring Notes`, `ATS`, `ATS Specifics`, `Date of Analysis`, plus a consolidated SharePoint-ready export covering all screening steps.

### Rating Scale

| Rating | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Core | Mandatory / Primary Requirement |
| 2 | Important | Strong Advantage / Frequently Mentioned |
| 3 | Supporting | Not required / Not mentioned / Nice-to-have |

**Exception — M (Leadership & People Management):**
- `1` = Must lead a team or hold direct reports
- `2` = Influential leadership without formal authority
- `3` = Nice-to-have / not mentioned

### JD Group Decision Rules

| Priority | Group Code | Group Name                          | Primary signal                                |
| -------- | ---------- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | TPM        | Transformation & Project Management | F or J rated 1                                |
| 2        | CSEO       | Chief of Staff & Executive Office   | A + B rated 1 with strong executive proximity |
| 3        | OSS        | Strategy & Corporate Development    | A rated 1                                     |
| 4        | SCD        | Operations & Shared Services        | C rated 1, G supporting                       |
| 5        | CFPA       | Controlling, FP&A & Finance         | D or E rated 1                                |
| 6        | POESG      | Procurement, Outsourcing & ESG      | H or I rated 1                                |

When two groups are equally dominant, assign the stronger as Primary and the weaker as Secondary. If no clear secondary group exists, leave `JD Group Secondary` blank.

**Important – JD Group Output Format**
When producing outputs in D.1 and D.2, always use the Group Name (not the Group Code).

## B. Execution Sequence

1. Read the JD `.md` file from
   `OneDrive > Obsidian Vault > JobSearch Camunda > Job Descriptions`
2. Rate all 17 Areas of Expertise against the JD content
3. Write Key Patterns and CV Tailoring Notes (2–4 sentences maximum)
4. Identify the ATS system
5. Assign Primary and Secondary JD Group
6. Produce the per-JD analysis block (Section D.1) including any confidence flags
7. Repeat steps 1–6 for each JD in the batch
8. Produce the end-of-batch SharePoint export (Section D.2)

**Language note:** JDs written in German are analysed in their original language. Areas of Expertise ratings apply to the role's requirements regardless of the language in which they are expressed.

## C. ATS Identification

The goal is to identify the **actual Applicant Tracking System (ATS)** used by the company for the specific Job Lead.

**Mandatory Rule:**

You **must** identify the real ATS by checking the application flow. Always follow this strict order of priority:

1. **Primary Source – Job Post Link (Mandatory first check)** Use the **Job Post Link** column from Job Hunting Lists.xlsx. This is the company’s official careers page for that specific role. This is the **preferred and most reliable** source.
2. **Secondary Source – LinkedIn (Only if Job Post Link is unavailable or insufficient)** Only use the LinkedIn job posting if:
    - The Job Post Link is missing, broken, or does not lead to an application page, **or**
    - The Job Description explicitly states that “applications are managed off LinkedIn”.

**Additional Rules:**

- If the Job Description says “Responses managed off LinkedIn” or similar wording, you **must still check the Job Post Link first**.
- Do **not** stop at LinkedIn if a functional Job Post Link exists.
- If neither source allows you to clearly identify the ATS, mark it as **Unknown** and add a note.
- When you identify the ATS, also record relevant **ATS Specifics** (e.g. formatting preferences, if known).

### D.2 End-of-Batch SharePoint Export

This section defines the exact output format to be used after completing Steps B.1 to B.4. 
This output should be generated **after completing Steps B.1 to B.4** for a Job Lead (or batch of Job Leads). It produces one consolidated row per Job Lead containing all relevant fields from the screening steps.
The goal is to produce one clean, copy-paste-ready row per Job Lead that can be directly inserted into the Job Leads SharePoint list (and mirrored in Job Hunting Lists.xlsx).
The output is rendered as an interactive HTML widget with a Copy button.


**Format:** tab-delimited with RFC 4180-style quoting

**Column sequence** — must match `Job Leads` in `[[Job Hunting Lists.xlsx]]`
exactly:

1. Title
2. ID
3. Position
4. Company
5. City
6. LinkedIn
7. Job Post Link
8. Roadblocks
9. Misalignments
10. JD Group Primary
11. JD Group Secondary
12–28. A · B · C · D · E · F · G · H · I · J · K · L · M · N · O · P · Q   ← Exactly 17 Area of Expertise columns
12. Key Patterns - CV Tailoring Notes
13. ATS
14. ATS Specifics
15. Date of Analysis
16. Number of Applications Received
17. Days Since Publication
18. Overall Fit Score



**Rules:**
- One header row, one data row per Job Lead
- Rows sorted by SharePoint ID descending (highest ID first)
- Multi-line text fields enclosed in double quotes with internal newlines
  preserved — e.g., `"Language: German (Verhandlungssicher)\nSeniority:
  Overqualified (targets 4–7 yrs)"`. Excel handles double-quoted fields
  with internal newlines correctly on paste.
- Empty cells represented as empty strings (two consecutive tabs)
- Widget includes two button
