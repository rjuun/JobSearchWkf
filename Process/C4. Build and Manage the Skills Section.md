## Purpose

Create and maintain the dedicated **Skills section** at the top of the CV (positioned between the Profile and Professional Experience). This section serves as the single source of truth for the candidate’s relevant skills for a specific Job Lead.

This step aims to:

- Provide a clear, well-organized, and ATS-friendly overview of the candidate’s skills.
- Ensure consistency between the skills mentioned in the Professional Experience section and the top Skills List.
- Improve both human readability and ATS performance by maintaining one authoritative Skills section.

---

## A. Execution Sequence

1. Retrieve **My Skills** from every Keep-gated row produced in [[C2. Map JD Requirements to Supporting Evidence]] — the candidate's own vocabulary for the evidence behind each Keep bullet. Every value here must appear in the Skills List (the mandatory Consistency Rule below). Top up the remaining slots with a requirement-overlap ranking across `tbl_Skills_Master`, `tbl_STAR_Competences` and `tbl_STAR_Attributes`.
2. Organize the skills into logical categories.
3. Apply the **Consistency Rule** between the top Skills List and the My Skills values from C2.
4. Review the Skills section for clarity, structure, and completeness.
5. Produce the final Skills section ready for use in **C6. Compile Complete CV Document**.

> [!Note]
> C4 does not source from the C3 bracketed tags (`Requirement Skills` — JD-facing, ATS-compatible language). It sources from **My Skills**, the candidate's own vocabulary, established at C2 from the matched evidence item. Requirement Skills stays bullet-level detail; it is not what the top Skills List is built from.

---

## B. Skills Section Construction Rules

### 1. Structure and Categorization

The Skills section should be organized into **logical categories** rather than presented as one long flat list. Categories should reflect the main capability areas relevant to the Job Lead.

**Recommended approach:**

- Group skills into 3–5 high-level categories.
- Place the most relevant categories (aligned with Core requirements) at the top.
- Keep each category concise (ideally 4–8 skills per category).

**Example structure:**

- Transformation & Process Excellence
- Leadership & Stakeholder Management
- Governance, Risk & Compliance
- Strategic Execution & Change

### 2. Consistency Rule (Mandatory)

Every **My Skills** value on a Keep row **must** appear in the top Skills List — this is unconditional, not subject to the category-size guidance above. Requirement Skills (the bracketed tag) is separate, JD-facing detail and is not what this rule checks.

**Rationale:**

- The top Skills List acts as the **single source of truth**.
- This ensures the CV remains consistent and professional.
- It prevents situations where a skill is referenced in the experience section but is missing from the main Skills overview.

### 3. Prioritization

- Prioritize skills that were associated with **Core** and **Important** requirements during C3. 
- Skills linked only to Nice-to-Have requirements can be included if space allows, but they are secondary.

### 4. Formatting Guidelines

- Use clear category headings.
- Keep skill names concise and consistent in style.
- Avoid overly long lists within each category.
- Ensure the section remains easy to scan for both human readers and ATS systems.

---

## C. Output

**Primary Output:** A structured **Skills section** ready to be inserted into the CV during **C6. Compile Complete CV Document**.

The output should include:

- Category headings
- Skills listed under each category
- Confirmation that the Consistency Rule has been applied

---

## D. Quality & Consistency Rules

- The top Skills List is the **master reference** for skills on the CV.
- Any skill referenced in the Professional Experience section must be present in the Skills List.
- Do not duplicate skills unnecessarily across categories.
- Maintain visual consistency in formatting (same style for all categories and skills).
- After building the Skills List, perform a final cross-check with the bullets from C3 to ensure alignment.
- The mandatory inclusions are every My Skills value from a Keep row — these go in regardless of whether they match `tbl_Skills_Master`. The top-up ranking beyond that does cross-reference `tbl_Skills_Master`, `tbl_STAR_Competences` and `tbl_STAR_Attributes` — that's the recognised vocabulary used to fill remaining slots.

---

### Notes

- Note: C4 does not pull skills directly from B5. Instead, it organizes the My Skills values already carried on each Keep row since C2, topped up by a ranking across the profile's Skills/Competences/Attributes tables. This ensures strong consistency between the Professional Experience section and the top Skills section.
- This step (C4) focuses exclusively on the **top Skills section** of the CV. The logic for associating skills _inside_ individual bullets is handled in **C3**.
- By making the top Skills List the single source of truth, we reduce the need to force skills into every bullet and improve overall CV clarity and professionalism.
- C4 should be executed after C3 (once the bullets and their associated skills are defined).
