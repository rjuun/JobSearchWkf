## Purpose

This step captures two objective data points for each Job Lead that feed directly into the Application Priority Scoring:

- **Posting Freshness**: how many days have passed since the position was first published (fresher is better)
- **Market Saturation**: how many people have already applied or clicked apply (lower is better)

These are lightweight, rule-based checks that require no profile analysis. They serve as a pre-filter before Roadblocks and deeper screening: a posting that is already stale and heavily saturated may not justify further investment.

---

## A. Data Source

Both data points are captured passively by the bookmarklet at the moment the Job Lead is stored. They appear as header noise at the top of every `.md` file in the `Job Descriptions` folder, in a format similar to:

> `Vienna, Vienna, Austria · 2 weeks ago · 80 people clicked apply`
> `Lisbon Metropolitan Area · 5 days ago · Over 100 applicants`
> `Vienna, Vienna, Austria · 4 months ago · Over 100 people clicked apply`

**No additional data collection step is required.** Claude reads this directly from the `.md` file during the Job Lead Screening session.

---

## B. Extraction and Conversion Rules

### 1. Days Since Publication

LinkedIn expresses posting age in relative terms. Always convert to an **integer number of days** regardless of the original format.

| LinkedIn format               | Conversion                                  |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| `X hours ago` / `today`       | 0 days                                      |
| `Yesterday`                   | 1 day                                       |
| `X days ago`                  | X days                                      |
| `1 week ago` / `a week ago`   | 7 days                                      |
| `X weeks ago`                 | X × 7 days                                  |
| `1 month ago` / `a month ago` | 30 days                                     |
| `X months ago`                | X × 30 days                                 |
| `Reposted X weeks ago`        | Use repost date, note original may be older |

**Reference anchor:** The relative time in the `.md` file reflects the state at bookmark time, recorded in the `Created` field of the SharePoint Job Leads item. If screening is conducted more than 7 days after capture, add the elapsed days to the converted value for a more accurate figure.

### 2. Number of Applications Received

Extract the number from phrases such as:
- `80 people clicked apply` → `80`
- `Over 100 people clicked apply` → `100+`
- `35 applicants` → `35`
- `Over 100 applicants` → `100+`

Record the value as shown. Use `100+` when LinkedIn uses the "Over 100" formulation — do not attempt to estimate beyond this.

### 3. Date of Analysis

Record the date on which screening is being executed, in `DD.MM.YYYY` format. 
This anchors the analysis in time for future reference.

## C. Interpretation Scale

These thresholds are indicative. For senior roles, "people who clicked apply" is a less reliable proxy for actual competition than for junior roles — many senior candidates are head-hunted and do not apply via LinkedIn. Apply judgment accordingly.

### Posting Freshness

| Days Since Publication | Assessment                                | Colour |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------ |
| 0 – 7 days             | 🟢 Very fresh — prioritise                | 2ecc71 |
| 8 – 21 days            | 🟡 Recent — still active                  | fff200 |
| 22 – 60 days           | 🟠 Aging — apply promptly                 | f5a623 |
| 61 – 120 days          | 🔴 Stale — risk of position being filled  |        |
| 120+ days              | ⛔ Likely dead — verify role is still open | ff1a1a |

### Market Saturation

| Applicants | Assessment                                  | Colour |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------ |
| < 30       | 🟢 Low competition                          | 2ecc71 |
| 30 – 99    | 🟡 Moderate competition                     | fff200 |
| 100+       | 🔴 High competition — senior filter applies | ff1a1a |

> [!NOTE] Senior Role Adjustment
> For Director-level and above positions, `100+` applicants does not carry the same weight as for mid-level roles. The realistic senior-qualified pool is typically a fraction of the click count. Weight this indicator accordingly — do not discard a role solely on the basis of `100+` applicants if freshness and fit are strong.

---

## C. Interpretation Scale

The visual interpretation of both indicators (colour coding and labels) is handled via **SharePoint Format Columns** applied directly to the `Days Since Publication` and `Number of Applications Received` columns in the Job Leads list. Do not replicate scale logic here.

The threshold values to configure in SharePoint are:

**Posting Freshness** (`Days Since Publication`)
- 0 – 7 → Very fresh
- 8 – 14 → Recent
- 15 – 30 → Aging
- 31 – 60 → Stale
- 60+ → Likely dead

**Market Saturation** (`Number of Applications Received`)
- < 30 → Low competition
- 30 – 99 → Moderate competition
- 100+ → High competition

## D. Output Procedure

For each Job Lead processed, record the following in the `Job Leads` 
SharePoint list (and its mirror in `Job Hunting Lists.xlsx`):

| Column                            | Value                       |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| `Days Since Publication`          | Integer (e.g., `14`, `120`) |
| `Number of Applications Received` | Integer or `100+`           |
| `Date of Analysis`                | `DD.MM.YYYY`                |
|                                   |                             |

If the header noise in the `.md` file does not contain posting age or applicant count (e.g., the data was not captured by the bookmarklet), record `N/A` and note the gap. Do not attempt to retrieve the data server-side — LinkedIn blocks automated access.

## E. Posting Viability Decision Gate

After recording `Days Since Publication` and `Number of Applications Received`, apply the following gate before proceeding to 
[[B2. Identify Roadblocks]]:

**If `Days Since Publication` ≥ 60:**

Place the Job Lead on **hold** and verify whether the position is still actively open before investing further screening effort. Do not proceed to Step 2 until viability is confirmed.

Verification options (in order of preference):
1. Check the original LinkedIn URL — if the posting is gone or shows "No longer accepting applications", mark as **Inactive**
2. Check the company's careers page directly for the same or equivalent role
3. If the posting shows **Reposted**, the role is actively being promoted again — treat as viable and proceed

**Recording the outcome:**
- Still active → proceed to [[B2. Identify Roadblocks]] and record `Posting Viability: Verified Active (DD.MM.YYYY)`
- No longer active → mark item as `Inactive` in the Job Leads list and remove from active screening queue
- Unable to verify → proceed with caution and record `Posting Viability: Unverified (DD.MM.YYYY)`

**If `Days Since Publication` < 60:** proceed directly to [[B2. Identify Roadblocks]] — no viability check required.

> [!NOTE] Reposted Postings
> When the JD header shows "Reposted X days/weeks ago", use the **repost date** as the freshness reference — the role is being actively re-promoted. Note that the applicant count displayed is **cumulative from the original posting**, not reset at repost time. This means market saturation for reposted roles is likely understated by the visible count.
